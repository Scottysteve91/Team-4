Mammals

The group of vertebrate animals in which the young are nourished with milk from special mammary glands of the mother.

Carnivores

A carnivore is an animal whose food and energy requirements derive from animal tissues (mainly muscle, fat and other soft tissues) whether through hunting or scavenging.

The carnivores we have in the Belfast Zoo are

Barbary Lion, Scottish Wildcat

Herbivores

A herbivore is an animal anatomically and physiologically adapted to eating plant material, for example foliage or marine algae, for the main component of its diet.

The Herbivores we have in the Belfast Zoo are

Asian Elephant, Capybara

Omnivores

An omnivore is an animal that has the ability to eat and survive on both plant and animal matter. Obtaining energy and nutrients from plant and animal matter, omnivores digest carbohydrates, protein, fat, and fiber, and metabolize the nutrients and energy of the sources absorbed.

The Herbivores we have in the Belfast Zoo are

Andean Bear, Cape Porcupine
